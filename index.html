About the Project

Air pollution is one of the most significant environmental challenges affecting both human health and ecosystems worldwide. It occurs when harmful substances such as gases, particulate matter, and chemical pollutants accumulate in the atmosphere at concentrations that negatively impact environmental and public health conditions. Major atmospheric pollutants including nitrogen dioxide (NO₂), particulate matter (PM10), and fine particulate matter (PM2.5) are commonly linked to transportation, industrial activities, fossil fuel combustion, and urban development.

Monitoring air pollution is essential for understanding environmental conditions, identifying high-risk areas, and supporting sustainable environmental management. Long-term exposure to atmospheric pollutants can contribute to respiratory diseases, cardiovascular complications, ecosystem degradation, and reduced air quality, particularly within densely populated and industrialized regions. As urban expansion and land cover changes continue to influence environmental systems, the need for effective spatial monitoring and analysis becomes increasingly important.

This project focuses on the geospatial analysis and visualization of air pollution and land cover dynamics across Austria using GIS and WebGIS technologies. The study investigates the spatial distribution and environmental impact of NO₂, PM10, and PM2.5 by integrating atmospheric pollution datasets, land cover information, and population exposure analysis. Through the use of GIS-based spatial analysis and interactive WebGIS visualization, the project demonstrates how geospatial technologies can support environmental monitoring, pollution assessment, and improved understanding of atmospheric conditions across Austria.
